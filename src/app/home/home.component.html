Karnaugh Map is the powerful tool for minimizing complex logic expressions or turning them into disjunctive or conjunctive normal form. The following website was created to help anyone get familiar with this tool by providing a number of exercises. You can choose between practice and quiz sections. The former presents many questions and give informative feedback on the go. The latter is for the ones who already feel confident with a concept of the Karnaugh Map and wish to try themselves by gaining the points.

If you have never heard of the Karnaugh Map or you are still not sure how to use it, start with the tutorial where you will find the explanation of the Karnaugh Map model step by step. You can also read a short article on the visual representation of the resolution rule in the Karnaugh map.

The website also provides the instant solver where you can find the minimal DNF or CNF for any logic expression or any Karnaugh Map with pre-marked states.
